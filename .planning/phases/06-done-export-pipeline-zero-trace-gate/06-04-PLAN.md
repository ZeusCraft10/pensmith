<objective>
Implement `bin/lib/exporter.ts` — the export module that satisfies the LOAD-BEARING zero-trace contract for EVERY format. `zeroTracePatch()` is the mandatory last step of every .docx export: it loads the ZIP in-process with JSZip, blanks every identifying field in docProps/core.xml + docProps/app.xml (the FULL Dublin-Core + cp + extended-property set), epochs the timestamps, sweeps EVERY non-binary entry (including non-.xml entries like _rels/.rels) for 'pensmith', and writes back atomically. `zeroTracePdf()` is the mandatory last step of every .pdf export: it loads the PDF in-process with pure-JS pdf-lib, empties the /Info document-info dictionary, removes the XMP metadata stream, and sweeps the bytes for any literal 'pensmith' — guaranteeing zero trace WITHOUT Pandoc or a PDF engine. `exportDraft()` orchestrates Pandoc shellout for .docx/.pdf/.tex/.md, emits .tex with NO generator comment, falls back to markdown-only with a banner when Pandoc/PDF-engine is absent (never throwing), and copies .paper/CITATIONS.bib alongside the output (DONE-08).

Purpose: DONE-07 + TEST-10 are THE gating non-negotiable (CLAUDE.md / ROADMAP SC1: "ZERO pensmith trace in ANY export ... THE gating test"). This is delivered for ALL FOUR formats — PDF is NOT scoped out and NOT deferred. Pandoc alone is insufficient: for .docx even `--metadata author=""` leaves the empty-but-present field and a source YAML header can inject a real name; for .pdf the `--variable pdfcreator=/pdfproducer=` flags CANNOT strip the XMP stream the engine injects (06-RESEARCH Pitfall 3). The in-process scrubs (JSZip for docx, pdf-lib for pdf) are the only deterministic guarantees. Because Pandoc + the PDF engine are ABSENT on the build machine, the zero-trace test (06-01) runs zeroTracePatch against the committed fixture .docx and zeroTracePdf against the committed fixture .pdf — this plan's scrubs MUST make those offline tests GREEN. exportDraft must recoup the isPandocPresent() check at call time (Pitfall 1) so a missing Pandoc degrades to md-only rather than crashing.
